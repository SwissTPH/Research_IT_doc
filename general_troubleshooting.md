# General Troubleshooting

## Check the official documentation.

If you already know the tools you want to use but aren’t sure where to begin, always start with the official instructions. Tool authors usually provide comprehensive guidance, so take the time to review them carefully. Use keyword searches to find what you need quickly.

## Use search engines to explore further.

Search engines can lead you to a wealth of valuable resources, including community discussions and troubleshooting tips. While search engines may not always be 100% accurate, forums like [StackOverflow](https://stackoverflow.com/) or [Biostars](https://www.biostars.org/) can provide helpful insights.

- StackOverflow: A general forum for coding and computer science questions.

- Biostars: Focused on bioinformatics and biostatistics topics.

Many developers maintain GitHub repositories where you can explore user discussions in the Issues or Discussions sections. Here, you might find answers to your questions directly from the tool authors or other users.

Other useful resources:

- [Swiss TPH eLearning platform](https://elearning.swisstph.ch/)

- [REDCap community](https://redcap.vumc.org/community/) (requires REDCap Community access)

- [ODK forum](https://forum.getodk.org/)

- [Coursera - free online courses](https://www.coursera.org/)

## Use AI tools wisely.

If you even don't know what tools to use or what questions to be searched, you might need a flash of inspiration. Sometimes you stuck in some questions which are actually not complex, but you need a trigger to light up a spark. Modern language models are there to help you.

You can start with some naive words, which in most of the time these language models are able to understand. You can even deepen your questions step by step. Here are some famous AI assistants with free versions to use:

- [ChatGPT](https://chatgpt.com/)

- [Claude](https://claude.ai/)

- [Perplexity](https://www.perplexity.ai/)

Depending on the settings, these AI applications may use your content to train the models. Normally there should be options to turn it off. For more information about privacy, please check the privacy policies according to your choice of AI applications.

There are also possibilities to use **open source AI alternatives**. Please go to [High Performance Computing](https://intranet.swisstph.ch/en/aoc/research-it/consulting-coordination-and-collaboration#c15966) for more information.

Caution: Do not rely on AI blindly. These tools are not researchers and lack independent reasoning. Always verify their outputs against credible references, especially for scientific work.

## Document your work

Keeping detailed records of your work is crucial, both for your own reference and for collaboration with colleagues. Here are some tools to help:

- [GitHub](https://github.com/): A developer platform that allows developers to create, store, manage and share their code.

- [Gist](https://gist.github.com/): Ideal for instantly sharing code, notes and snippets.

- [GitLab](https://about.gitlab.com/): A web-based platform for collaboration on large and complex projects.

- [Confluence](https://swisstph.atlassian.net/): Used at Swiss TPH to document team works and maintain wiki pages for knowledge sharing.

- [Notion](https://www.notion.com/): A connect workspace that provides free Education Plus Plan for students and educators.

- [MarkText](https://github.com/marktext/marktext): A simple [markdown](https://www.markdownguide.org/cheat-sheet/) editor, available for Linux, macOS and Windows.
